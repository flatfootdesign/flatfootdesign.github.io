Cars.com

Cars.com is one of the largest digital marketplaces for connecting consumers with auto dealers in their area. Users can research, search, and review different vehicles near them and reach out to dealers to view the vehicle IRL. Cars.com came to Eight Bit to help them create the first iteration of their mobile consumer-to-consumer marketplace for selling vehicles on mobile. This allows any user of Cars.com to sell their vehicle to any other user (or auto dealer) on the app or website.

When I joined the mobile team, the first web iteration of the consumer-to-consumer selling experience had just released, but there was little planning for how this feature would translate to mobile apps. My role on the team was to stabilize our workflow by creating a design delivery process and method of prioritizing needs from the organization. Once that was established, I designed the mobile version of this feature with major IA and interaction improvements that made their way back into the web version of the feature. I collaborated with various stakeholders throughout the process such as developers, product managers, web designers, and SMEs to optimize and iterate on the experience.

Initial feature landing. The experience is largely a form, so we focused on how we can make it easier and faster to complete. We implemented features like auto-complete, input suggestions, progressive disclosure, and a vehicle lookup. We also wanted to make sure that the flow felt natural and that information was group and structured in a logical way.

Updated profile and vehicle listings page. I redesigned the user profile to be a better container of their activity and interactions. I also created the view for user-created listings where users could edit, update, and publish their listings.
